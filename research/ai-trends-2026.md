# Research AI Trends (Task 60)

Date: 2026-03-05
Scope: Current trends in AI and related technologies (2025-2026 signals)

## Method

- Prioritized primary sources: vendor announcements, regulator pages, standards/docs, and research reports.
- Used late-2024 through 2026 sources, emphasizing 2025-2026 updates.
- Interpreted "current" as trends active as of March 5, 2026.

## Current Trends

1. Agentic AI is moving from chat to action-oriented workflows.
   - Why this is current: Major model providers now position agent behavior (tool use, browser actions, multi-step execution) as a core capability, not an add-on.
   - Sources:
     - OpenAI, "Introducing OpenAI o3 and o4-mini" (April 16, 2025): https://openai.com/index/introducing-o3-and-o4-mini/
     - OpenAI, "Introducing ChatGPT agent" (2025): https://openai.com/index/introducing-chatgpt-agent/
     - Google, "Introducing Gemini 2.0 ... for the agentic era" (Dec 11, 2024): https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/google-deepmind/google-gemini-ai-update-december-2024/

2. Reasoning-focused models ("thinking" or hybrid reasoning modes) are a leading frontier.
   - Why this is current: Flagship launches emphasize reasoning depth, controllable thinking time, and benchmark gains in math/coding/science.
   - Sources:
     - OpenAI o3/o4-mini launch (Apr 16, 2025): https://openai.com/index/introducing-o3-and-o4-mini/
     - Anthropic Claude 3.7 Sonnet launch (Feb 24, 2025): https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-3-7-sonnet
     - DeepSeek-R1 paper (arXiv, 2025; revised 2026): https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.12948

3. Multimodal-native systems (text, vision, audio, tools) are becoming default product architecture.
   - Why this is current: Frontline models explicitly combine multimodal IO with tool use and multi-step planning.
   - Sources:
     - Google Gemini 2.0 announcement: https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/google-deepmind/google-gemini-ai-update-december-2024/
     - OpenAI o3/o4-mini ("thinking with images", multimodal reasoning): https://openai.com/index/introducing-o3-and-o4-mini/

4. Very long context is becoming practical for enterprise coding and document workloads.
   - Why this is current: API providers now expose 1M-token context options for production organizations.
   - Sources:
     - Anthropic Claude docs, context windows (1M token context window): https://platform.claude.com/docs/en/build-with-claude/context-windows

5. Model capability is improving while cost/efficiency barriers are dropping.
   - Why this is current: Benchmark performance, inference affordability, and smaller-model competitiveness improved sharply in recent cycles.
   - Sources:
     - Stanford HAI, AI Index 2025 ("AI becomes more efficient, affordable and accessible"): https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2025-ai-index-report

6. Enterprise AI adoption is now broad, but most companies still struggle to scale and capture full EBIT impact.
   - Why this is current: Usage is high, but scaling and enterprise-level value capture lag.
   - Sources:
     - McKinsey, "The state of AI in 2025" (Nov 5, 2025): https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai

7. AI-assisted software development is now baseline behavior for many developers.
   - Why this is current: GitHub reports strong AI repo growth, high LLM SDK usage, and rapid Copilot uptake among new developers.
   - Sources:
     - GitHub Octoverse 2025 (updated Feb 28, 2026): https://github.blog/news-insights/octoverse/octoverse-a-new-developer-joins-github-every-second-as-ai-leads-typescript-to-1/

8. On-device AI (AI PCs / edge AI compute) is entering mainstream hardware planning cycles.
   - Why this is current: Analyst forecasts show rapid share gains for AI-capable PCs through 2025-2026.
   - Sources:
     - Gartner press release (Aug 28, 2025): https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-08-28-gartner-says-artificial-intelligence-pcs-will-represent-31-percent-of-worldwide-pc-market-by-the-end-of-2025

9. AI infrastructure buildout is accelerating, with supply-chain localization and manufacturing as strategic priorities.
   - Why this is current: NVIDIA reports Blackwell production ramp milestones and US-based manufacturing expansion during 2025.
   - Sources:
     - NVIDIA (Apr 14, 2025): https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/nvidia-manufacture-american-made-ai-supercomputers-us/
     - NVIDIA (Oct 17, 2025): https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/tsmc-blackwell-manufacturing/

10. AI governance is shifting from policy drafting to phased enforcement and operational compliance.
   - Why this is current: The EU AI Act timeline moved into live obligations in 2025, with broader applicability in 2026+.
   - Sources:
     - European Commission digital policy page (AI Act timeline and obligations): https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai
     - EU AI Act Service Desk timeline: https://ai-act-service-desk.ec.europa.eu/en/ai-act/timeline/timeline-implementation-eu-ai-act

11. Energy and grid impacts of AI are now a first-order planning issue.
   - Why this is current: The IEA reports rapid data-center demand growth tied to AI and highlights power-system implications to 2030.
   - Sources:
     - IEA, Energy and AI (2025): https://www.iea.org/reports/energy-and-ai
     - IEA Executive Summary with demand figures: https://www.iea.org/reports/energy-and-ai/executive-summary

## Notes

- Trends are directional and may evolve quickly across quarters.
- This list intentionally focuses on evidence-backed signals from primary or institutionally authoritative sources.
